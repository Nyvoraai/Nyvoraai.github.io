Is Claude AI Down Today? Here's How to Check (And What to Do)

If you typed a prompt into Claude and got nothing back — a stuck cursor, a blank reply, or a flat-out error — you're probably here for one reason: you want to know, right now, whether the problem is on your end or Anthropic's. You're not alone. "Is Claude AI down today" has turned into one of the most-searched AI status questions of 2026, and there's a real reason for that. Here's everything worth knowing, plus exactly how to check for yourself in under a minute.

QUICK ANSWER

Short answer: probably not completely down — but Claude has gone through a genuinely rough stretch of short, scattered service hiccups throughout June 2026, rather than one long blackout. If something feels broken right now, the fastest way to get a real answer instead of a guess is Anthropic's official status page, which we'll walk through below.

How to Check if Claude AI Is Down Right Now

The biggest mistake people make when Claude won't respond is assuming the worst before actually checking. Outages do happen, but so do far more boring explanations — a flaky Wi-Fi connection, a browser extension blocking a script, or a stale cache. Before you panic, run through these four checks. Together they take less than two minutes.

  1. 1
    Check Anthropic's official status page. Anthropic runs a public status page that breaks Claude down by component — claude.ai, the Claude API, Claude Code, Claude Cowork, the developer console, and Claude for Government — so you can see exactly which piece is affected instead of a vague "everything's broken" message.
  2. 2
    Check Downdetector. Anthropic's own status page can lag a few minutes behind reality, especially right as an issue starts. Downdetector aggregates real-time, crowd-sourced reports and is often the first place a spike shows up — sometimes before Anthropic posts anything.
  3. 3
    Search social media. A quick search for "Claude down" on X usually surfaces real complaints within seconds of an issue starting. It won't explain the technical cause, but it instantly tells you whether you're alone or part of a wider wave.
  4. 4
    Rule out your own setup. If the status page and Downdetector both look clean but Claude still isn't working for you, the issue is probably local. Try a different browser, the Claude mobile app, or a quick refresh before blaming Anthropic.

Why Does Claude Keep Going Down? A Look at June 2026

If it feels like Claude has been unusually shaky lately, that's not just a feeling. Independent monitoring services tracking claude.ai have logged a noticeably dense run of incidents through early-to-mid June 2026 — short, warning-level slowdowns plus a handful of more visible outages, several clustered within the same two-week stretch. Most were measured in minutes to a few hours rather than full-day blackouts, and they tended to hit specific components rather than the whole platform at once — sometimes a particular model, sometimes just login or voice mode.

It's a pattern that lines up with something bigger: explosive demand. Claude's user base, API traffic, and coding-tool usage have all grown fast in 2026, and rapid growth is notoriously hard on infrastructure. Anthropic hasn't published detailed public post-mortems for most of these individual incidents, but the company has acknowledged in statements to outlets covering the story that scaling has contributed to recent reliability problems, particularly around coding-tool performance.

The practical takeaway: if Claude is acting up today, it's far more likely a short, component-specific hiccup that clears up within an hour or two than a sign of a major, prolonged failure. It's also worth keeping some perspective here. Every major AI provider — not just Anthropic — has dealt with reliability bumps as demand for chat and coding assistants has exploded industry-wide. Rapid scaling, new model rollouts, and surging concurrent usage are a difficult combination for any infrastructure team to manage perfectly. None of that makes a mid-task interruption less annoying in the moment, but it does help explain why "is Claude down" spikes in search interest tend to cluster around periods of heavy product growth rather than appearing completely at random.

Which Claude Components Are Most Often Affected?

Not every "Claude is down" report means the same thing, because Claude isn't really one single service — it's several. Knowing which piece tends to break helps you understand what's actually happening:

  • claude.ai (the web app): the most visible component, and the one most people mean when they say "Claude is down." Issues here often show up as failed logins, blank responses, or chats that won't load.
  • Claude Code: the developer-focused coding tool has seen its own share of separate incidents, sometimes independent of the main web app. If you're coding and hitting errors but claude.ai loads fine in another tab, this is usually the culprit.
  • Claude API: used by developers building products on top of Claude. API issues can ripple out to dozens of third-party apps at once, even when claude.ai itself looks completely fine.
  • Specific models: sometimes the issue isn't the platform at all — it's elevated errors on one particular model, like Opus, while Sonnet and Haiku keep working normally. Switching models in the dropdown can be a quick workaround.

This is exactly why the official status page breaks things out by component instead of giving one blanket "up" or "down" answer — and why it's worth checking the specific piece you're using rather than assuming the whole platform is affected.

Wait — Is This the Same as the Mythos/Fable 5 Suspension?

Not quite — and it's worth separating the two. Around mid-June 2026, Anthropic suspended access to its Claude Mythos 5 and Claude Fable 5 models in response to an export control directive. That's a policy and compliance decision, not a technical failure.

If you're specifically trying to reach one of those models and getting blocked, that's not "Claude is down" — that's access to a specific tier being intentionally paused. Anthropic has said it expects availability to return, though no confirmed restoration date has been published as of this writing.

Everything else — claude.ai, Claude Sonnet, Claude Opus, the standard chat experience most people use day to day — runs on a completely separate set of systems and isn't affected by that suspension. So if your version of Claude is working fine but one specific advanced model isn't available, that's the export-control suspension talking, not an outage.

What to Do If Claude Is Down

Once you've confirmed there's an actual issue and it's not just your setup, here's how to make the wait less annoying:

  1. 1
    Check which component is affected. If only Claude Code is down but claude.ai works fine, switch to the web app for anything urgent.
  2. 2
    Save your work locally. If you're mid-conversation on something important, copy your draft somewhere safe. Sessions can occasionally drop during an incident.
  3. 3
    Don't retry aggressively. Rapid repeated retries don't speed up a fix, and can worsen rate-limiting for everyone, including you.
  4. 4
    Subscribe to status updates. Anthropic's status page offers email and webhook subscriptions, so you're notified the moment something changes instead of refreshing manually.
  5. 5
    Have a backup plan. If you rely on Claude for time-sensitive work, know your fallback in advance — another tool, a teammate, or simply rescheduling.
  6. 6
    Give it time. Based on the incident pattern tracked by independent monitors, most issues resolve within an hour or two, often faster.

How Long Do Outages Usually Last?

Looking at the incident history logged by independent monitors, most individual Claude incidents in 2026 have resolved in well under three hours — many in under 30 minutes. The exceptions tend to be deeper infrastructure issues rather than the routine, more common slowdowns. So if you check the status page and see an active incident, the realistic expectation is usually "back soon," not "down for the day."

Should You Switch to a Different AI Tool During an Outage?

For a quick, time-sensitive task, sure — having a backup tool ready isn't a betrayal of loyalty, it's just practical. But it's worth resisting the urge to fully migrate every time there's a short hiccup. Looking at the incident pattern, most issues clear up faster than it would take to properly switch workflows, re-create custom instructions, or rebuild a project's context somewhere else. The more sensible move for regular users is keeping a lightweight backup option in mind for genuine emergencies, while letting routine short blips resolve on their own. For developers building production systems on the API, the calculation is different — and that's where status subscriptions and automatic failover routing (offered by several third-party monitoring tools) genuinely earn their keep.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Claude AI down right now?

The only way to know for certain in real time is to check Anthropic's official status page directly. It's run by Anthropic's own team and breaks status down by individual component, including claude.ai, the API, and Claude Code.

How can I tell if it's just me or a wider outage?

If the status page shows all systems operational but Claude still isn't working for you, the issue is most likely local — try a different browser, clear your cache, or check your internet connection before assuming it's on Anthropic's side.

Why has Claude been going down so much in 2026?

Independent monitors tracked a cluster of short, component-specific incidents through June 2026, lining up with a period of fast user and traffic growth. Anthropic has acknowledged that scaling has strained infrastructure at times.

Is the Fable 5 and Mythos 5 suspension the same thing as Claude being down?

No. That's a deliberate access suspension tied to an export control directive, separate from technical outages. Standard Claude models like Sonnet and Opus aren't affected by it.

What should I do while I wait for Claude to come back online?

Save any in-progress work, avoid spamming retries, subscribe to status page updates if you check often, and keep an eye on Downdetector for real-time confirmation that the issue is resolving.
